1. Educational Evaluation is required or not?	
Yes, It is good to have the evaluation as it will avoid RFE

2. What is the USCIS fee for premium processing?	$2,805

3. How long does USCIS usually take to process H1B cases (regular vs premium)?
Regular: 2-6 months; Premium: 15 calendar days.

4. USCIS fee for the H4 and H4 EAD filing? $570

5. What is the timeline for filing an H1B petition?
Cap-subject: Registration in March, filing begins April 1. Cap-exempt: Anytime.

6. When should we start preparing the petition?	
Ideally 2-3 months in advance to gather documents and review strategy.


7. What should be included in the job description to meet USCIS standards?
Clearly defined duties requiring at least a bachelor’s degree.


8. Do you review or draft the employer support letter and other filing documents?
Yes, most attorneys prepare or review all supporting documents.


9. Can you guide us through the LCA (Labor Condition Application) process?
Yes, it's a required step and attorneys typically handle the filing.


10. What are the most common reasons for an RFE or denial in H1B cases?
Specialty occupation doubt, wage level, or employer-employee relationship issues.

11. How do you handle an RFE if we receive one?	
The attorney will draft a detailed response addressing all USCIS concerns.


12. Can we appeal or refile if the petition is denied?	
Yes, you may appeal, file a motion to reopen, or refile depending on the reason.


13. What are the requirements for filing an H1B amendment?
if there is a material change in duties or work location.

14. What is the process for extending an H1B visa?
Similar to the initial petition. Can be filed up to 6 months before expiry.

15. What happens if there’s a gap in employment or change in work location?
It must be reported. Amendments or new petitions may be required.


16. Can H4 dependents study or work in the U.S.?	
They can study; can work only with an H4 EAD.

17. What are your legal fees and what do they include?	
Fees vary; typically include preparation, filing, and RFE response.

18. What are the employer’s obligations under H1B regulations?
Pay the required wage, notify USCIS of changes, and cover return costs if terminated early.

19. What happens if my job is terminated while on H1B?
You have a 60-day grace period to transfer, change status, or leave the U.S.


20. How much time do I have to find a new employer or change status?
You typically have a 60-day grace period or until I-94 expiry.

21. How do I maintain valid H1B status in the U.S.?	
Work only for your petitioning employer, follow immigration terms, and renew on time.

22. Can I travel internationally while my H1B is pending?
Generally not advisable. It could affect petition processing.


23. Can I transfer my H1B to another employer? What’s the process?
Yes, the new employer must file a petition. You may start work upon receipt.

23. What documents do I (and my employer) need to provide?
Resumes, degrees, job offer, employer tax info, and support letters.
